Natsu Ozawa is a Japanese computer science student at the University of Edinburgh and the University of Pennsylvania with a mission to make fast, data-driven, smart decisions ubiquitous. Natsu is joining Palantir Technologies in the summer of 2023 to take the next step in this ambition.

Despite being fantacized as a technologically advanced country, Japan struggles in the digital era, and many aspects of our society are still designed for the past. The problems Japan is facing inspire Natsu on the incredible things we can achieve in the next decade. We need to create systems and socities that embrace the complexity and connectivity of the modern world.

Natsu started programming when he was 15. Initially focusing on frontend and backend web development, he worked as a software engineer intern in multiple startup companies. As a university student, his technical interests broadened to computational social choice theory, category theory, scalable computer systems, functional programming, machine learning, and computer algorithms.

Aside from his commitment to smart decisions, Natsu is vocal in Plurality, Japanese digital policy, and Effective Altruism.
